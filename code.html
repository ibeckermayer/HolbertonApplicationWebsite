Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software

Amazon

I'm sure that most Holberton applicants have a story about what got them interested in the world of computers. For me, it was a question that I couldn't escape: "How can pressing keys on a keyboard actually mean anything to this chunk of metal sitting in front of me?" The internet is full of superficial answers like "computers think in 1's and 0's!" but we all know that still begs the question: "How can it 'think'?"

My thirst couldn't be quenched, until finally I bought this book by Charles Petzold which is an absolute masterpiece of explanatory writing. Petzold takes the reader from braille and telegraph relays to a full on operating system and high level software languages, and touches everything in between. I cannot recommend this book enough to anybody who really needs to know what's going on under the hood. No previous knowledge is required, and the reader will come away with a much deeper understanding for the apparent "magic" that runs the modern world.
